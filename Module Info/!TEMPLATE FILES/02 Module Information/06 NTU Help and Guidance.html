NTU Help and Guidance

It is important that you feel supported by the university during your studies. Below you will find information and guidance that will help you throughout your journey as a student at NTU.

Academic support

Academic writing

Attend an online library webinar or book a one-to-one appointment with the Library Learning and Teaching Team to improve skills such as academic writing, referencing and plagiarism. You can also take a look at the A-Z list of library workshop materials.

Learning resources

Find important links, resources and services such as Office 365, SoftwareHub, deadlines and timetables at MyNTU. This area aims to support your learning experience for all face-to-face, blended and online aspects of your studies. 

Technical support

If you are experiencing technical issues, please contact the Library Learning and Teaching Team. They offer phone and email support as well as providing guidance via the Student Help Learning Room in NOW.

Health and wellbeing

MyNTU

MyNTU is your dashboard where you can access a number of tools and services that enable you to complete your daily tasks with ease. You'll see a number of tiles that take you to your most commonly used tools including the Student Dashboard, your attendance, emails, NOW learning and more. 

Student community

In addition to your studies, it is important you explore what university life at NTU has to offer. Check out the NTU Life section within the StudentHub to join a social, attend an online gig or event, interact with NTU's global community and lots more.

Mental health and wellbeing

We understand the importance of looking after our mental health. Our Student Support Advisers are here to help and advise you with any problems you are facing. Book an appointment with an adviser or go to the Health and Mental Wellbeing pages within MyNTU to access the services and resources available to support you during your studies.

NTU offers a range of sessions, activities and experiences to promote wellbeing helping you to feel part of the NTU student community. Head over to the StudentHub to find out how to get involved.

Further support

The Student Handbook covers regulations, procedures and policies which you may need to refer to as a current student at NTU.

Sometimes circumstances beyond your control affect your academic performance. A Notification of Extenuating Circumstances (NEC) form allows you to inform the University. Go to the student handbook section of StudentHub for more information on NEC's, R4R's and how to submit an appeal.

Student dashboard

The Dashboard is an award-winning learning analytics resource for students and staff. This online tool is designed to help you understand how well you are engaging with your studies, which we know is a strong predictor of success. It also enables you to compare yourself with other students on your course. More information and guides on how to use the dashboard can be found at Your Student Dashboard

Accessibility

Accessibility statement

NTU is committed to ensuring that the platforms and resources that we use with our staff and students are accessible. Take a look at the NOW Accessibility Statement to learn more.

Reporting a problem

If you need to report accessibility problems with NOW or content hosted on NOW. In the first instance, contact your Module Leader to determine whether resources can be provided to you in an alternative format if needed, or whether enhancements can be made to your learning rooms where required. 

If you are having difficulties accessing specific features within NOW, please contact the Library Learning and Teaching Team.
