Learning Room Accessibility Statement

About accessibility

At NTU we endeavour to ensure that no students are disadvantaged when it comes to accessing the systems and resources we use. We understand that students may require assistive technologies, or prefer to engage in content on their own devices or in their own way. We cater for this by putting accessibility at the forefront of our thoughts when designing, creating or selecting materials to share with you.

Roadmap to improved accessibility

Whilst every effort is made to ensure all the materials we provide you are accessible, there may be some occasions where it is not possible to make a resource entirely accessible. This could be related to the time needed to do so, the availability of technologies such as Optical Character Recognition (OCR) software, or the caption accuracy on videos. There may also be third party resources needed or used within this Learning Room that aren't fully compliant and which we cannot modify.

These items listed below will be improved in due course:

  • Item 1 (e.g. Lecture Capture recordings with no captions or poor accuracy)
  • Item 2 (e.g. PowerPoint presentations with multiple images that require Alt text adding)
  • Item 3 (e.g. PDF scans of hand-written notes that have not been OCR'd)
